# Impeccable — design context

Synthesized from the repo (`global.css`, layout, components), `README.md`, and `AGENTS.md`. Update this file when brand or UX direction changes.

## Design Context

### Users

- **Primary:** Pastors and conference organizers booking paid speaking (keynotes, Saturday seminars, multi-day parish missions). They need clarity on format, credibility, and a low-friction path to inquire (form first).
- **Secondary:** Email subscribers (Kit list growth), podcast listeners (*The Cale Clarke Show* on Apple / Spotify / YouTube), pilgrimage interest, and donors to The Faith Explained Institute.
- **Context:** Mostly desktop research + mobile share; decisions are often “bookmark and return” or “forward to committee.” The site should feel trustworthy and calm, not salesy.

### Brand Personality

- **Voice:** First person as **Cale Clarke** (not “we” for the personal brand layer).
- **Tone:** Warm, clear, intellectually serious without jargon; room for **wonder and humor**; respectful of the Church and the room.
- **Three-word handle (inferred):** *Clear, human, credible.*  
  (Confirm or replace with Cale’s own three words when you have them.)
- **Emotional goal:** Confidence that the faith is intelligible and livable; invitation to act (subscribe, listen, book, give) without pressure.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Visual tone:** Editorial personal brand — warm paper/cream field, **slate-blue hero band** (monochrome blue stack: `--hero-blue-deep` → `--hero-blue`), not SaaS neon or stark minimal.
- **Typography:** **Cormorant Garamond** (display / headlines), **Source Serif 4** (body), **Source Sans 3** (UI, nav, buttons, forms). Hierarchy is classical and readable; body ~45–68ch via `--prose-width`.
- **Shape & depth:** **Pill buttons** (`border-radius: 999px`), cards with `--radius-lg` (14px), **soft shadows** (`--shadow-soft`, `--shadow-lift`), subtle gradients on section bands—not flat Material.
- **Theme:** **Light mode only** in the current system; no dark tokens yet.
- **References (historical intent in code):** “Kent-style” hero clarity; earlier inspiration included Sahil-style speaking clarity and full-bleed hero — keep **restraint** and Catholic context, not startup hype.
- **Anti-patterns to avoid:** Harvestable email in footer; prominent Relevant Radio archive links; generic “AI slop” layouts; breaking focus rings; gray body text on saturated hero without sufficient contrast (hero uses light text on dark overlay).

### Accessibility & Motion

- **Focus:** Global and component-level `:focus-visible` (accent outline); hero/nav uses light outlines on dark bar where needed.
- **Touch:** Prefer **~44–48px** minimum tap targets on primary actions (nav, CTAs, form submit).
- **Motion:** `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` short-circuits transitions/animations globally; hero entrance respects this.
- **Target (inferred):** Aim for **WCAG AA** for text contrast on cream and card surfaces; hero text on photo relies on gradient overlay — preserve or strengthen overlay if imagery changes.

### Design Principles

1. **Tokens, not one-offs** — Use `--color-*`, `--space-*`, `--font-*`, `--radius-*`, `--shadow-*` from `src/styles/global.css` before adding new hex values.
2. **Conversion order is content order** — Newsletter → podcast → speaking matches stated priorities; don’t bury the list below the fold without intent.
3. **Forms over exposed email** — Primary contact paths are Kit + direct Static Forms POST; keep inbox out of static chrome where possible.
4. **One hero, one portrait** — Full-bleed hero asset vs. speaking-column blazer (`cale-hero.jpg`) serve different crops; don’t reuse blindly without adjusting `object-position` / overlay.
5. **Credibility in the margins** — Institute + Cardinal sidebar support trust; keep copy accurate and permissions-respecting for imagery and titles.
6. **Polish pass checklist** — For UI changes: consistent hover/focus/active on interactive elements, spacing scale alignment, and reduced-motion safety.
